Design rules

Viscosity Across Ionic-Liquid Parameter Space

Presented at the APS March Meeting (Las Vegas, 2023) · builds on J. Chem. Phys. 163, 044503 (2025)

The question

If you could turn one molecular "knob" to make an ionic liquid less viscous, which knob should it be? Experiments can only compare the chemistries that get synthesized; a coarse-grained model lets us vary one property at a time, cleanly, across ranges no single family of compounds can span.

What we did

Using the validated Stockmayer framework, we systematically varied electric charge, dipole moment and dipole mobility, ion size, and chain length, computing viscosity for each point in parameter space with Green–Kubo methods in LAMMPS. The automated Python/Slurm workflow made it practical to cover hundreds of state points on HPC resources.

What we found

Each molecular property leaves a distinct fingerprint on the viscosity, and separating them isolates concrete, molecular-level design rules for engineering low-viscosity ionic liquids. Obtaining this kind of guidance in the lab would be prohibitively expensive, since it would mean synthesizing and measuring candidate compounds one at a time.
