Computational Soft Matter · Polymer Physics

Jester (Jes) N. Itliong

Physics PhD Candidate, Michigan Technological University

I study how molecular-scale properties — charge, dipole moment, ion size — govern the macroscopic viscosity of ionic liquids and polymerized ionic liquids, using coarse-grained molecular dynamics simulations (LAMMPS), Python, and HPC/Slurm pipelines. I'm advised by Prof. Issei Nakamura.

My dissertation, “Molecular Origins of Viscosity in Ionic and Polymerized Ionic Liquids: Insights from Stockmayer Fluid Simulations,” is scheduled for defense on July 27, 2026.

Research

My work connects molecular interactions to macroscopic transport in charged soft matter. I run equilibrium and non-equilibrium molecular dynamics simulations and analyze them with Green–Kubo, Einstein–Helfand, and correlation-function methods, in collaboration with researchers at Sandia National Laboratories.

Model validation

A coarse-grained Stockmayer model for ethylammonium nitrate

Parameterized a minimal dipolar model of the protic ionic liquid EAN (σ⁺ = 4.80 Å, q = +0.35e, μ = 5.17 D) and showed that it reproduces EAN's experimental viscosity and glass-transition behavior — evidence that a simple coarse-grained fluid can capture the transport physics of a real ionic liquid.

Design rules

Viscosity across ionic-liquid parameter space

Systematically varied charge, dipole moment and mobility, ion size, and chain length to map how each molecular property governs viscosity — isolating molecular-level design rules for engineering low-viscosity ionic liquids.

Read more →

Polymer dynamics

Polymerized ionic liquids: scaling, chain dynamics, ion pairing

Examined how cation size and dipole mobility affect viscosity scaling, ion-pair dynamics, and chain orientational relaxation in polymerized ionic liquids (polyILs), linking monomer-scale chemistry to melt rheology.
