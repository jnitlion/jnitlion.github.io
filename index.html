Computational Soft Matter · Polymer Physics

Jester (Jes) N. Itliong

Physics PhD Candidate, Michigan Technological University

Mabuhay! I'm Jes, a Ph.D. candidate in Physics with expertise in multiscale molecular simulations of polymers, ionic liquids, and soft materials. I am experienced in atomistic and coarse-grained molecular dynamics, statistical mechanics, and high-performance scientific computing to investigate transport, rheological, dielectric, and structural properties of complex soft-matter systems. I have over 8 years of interdisciplinary computational research experience and more than 4 years of university teaching.

My Ph.D. focuses on how molecular-scale properties such as charge, dipole moment and mobility, ion size, and chain length govern the macroscopic viscosity of ionic liquids and polymerized ionic liquids, using coarse-grained molecular dynamics simulations (LAMMPS). My dissertation, “Molecular Origins of Viscosity in Ionic and Polymerized Ionic Liquids: Insights from Stockmayer Fluid Simulations,” is scheduled for defense on July 27, 2026.

I enjoy developing mini-projects (simulator, games, machine learning) and writing blogs during my spare time. I like fishes (my partner and I maintain a 75-gal aquarium with 3 little fishes) and plants. I am also into sports like volleyball and fitness.

Research

My work connects molecular interactions to macroscopic transport in charged soft matter. I run equilibrium and non-equilibrium molecular dynamics simulations and analyze them with Green–Kubo, Einstein–Helfand, and time-correlation-function methods.

Model validation

A coarse-grained Stockmayer model for ethylammonium nitrate

Parameterized a minimal charged and dipolar model of the protic ionic liquid EAN and showed that it reproduces EAN's structural and transport properties as well as the glass-transition behavior. This proves that a simple coarse-grained fluid can capture the physics of a real ionic liquid.

Read more →

Design rules

Viscosity across ionic-liquid parameter space

Systematically varied charge, dipole moment, and ion size to map how each molecular property governs viscosity. This provides molecular-level design rules for engineering low-viscosity ionic liquids.

Read more →

Polymer dynamics

Polymerized ionic liquids: scaling, chain dynamics, ion pairing

Examined how cation size and dipole mobility affect viscosity (and its chain-length scaling), ion-pair dynamics, and chain orientational relaxation in polymerized ionic liquids (polyILs), linking monomer-scale physics to melt rheology.
